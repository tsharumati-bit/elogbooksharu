Daily Tasks
  • 08 Apr, Tue
    Prepared audit working papers — worked on two main files: the General Ledger (GL) extract and the Revenue Lead Schedule.

    General Ledger (STW GL):
    Extracted all transactions under account (Misc Income – Upkeep, Maintenance & Security) from the main STW ledger and organised them into a dedicated worksheet following the same format as existing GL sub-schedules. Organised the (Factory Rental) worksheet with proper structured formatting including SUM formula totals rows. Performed a company-level analysis of account, identifying 16 tenants along with their transaction counts, total credits and total debits — flagging naming inconsistencies for Hong Yun Aluminium across multiple entries.

    Revenue Lead Schedule:
    Updated the Factory Rental section with current year (FY2026) invoice dates and invoice numbers by cross-referencing GL data for all tenants. Ensured invoice details, monthly rental amounts, duration (months), and total rental calculations were consistent with GL entries. Maintained all existing formulas, formatting, and cross-sheet references throughout both files.
  • 07 Apr – 10 Apr
    Reviewed and updated the Property Development Cost working paper for — cross-referenced the trial balance, general ledger, and supporting schedules to ensure all figures were accurate and complete.

    Missing Account Codes:
    Identified account codes recorded in the trial balance that were not yet reflected in the working paper and added the missing codes under their respective project groups, ensuring classification was consistent with the existing format. For newly added codes with no prior year balance, figures were recorded as current period additions with the carried-forward balance set accordingly.

    Reconciliation & Corrections:
    Reviewed existing entries where the carried-forward balance and trial balance columns were not in agreement, traced each discrepancy to specific account codes and corrected the values to ensure totals reconcile. Updated the subtotal rows for each project group in the current year movement section, ensuring each project's closing balance ties to the corresponding trial balance figure.

    Movement Schedule Completeness:
    Verified that all additions, reclassifications, and reversals were properly captured so that the movement schedule is complete and accurate for the reporting period.
What I Learned
This week deepened my understanding of audit working paper preparation across two key areas. From the GL and Revenue Lead Schedule work, I learned how rental income is systematically traced from the general ledger to the lead schedule, and how even minor naming inconsistencies must be flagged. The Property Development Cost working paper taught me how auditors reconcile a movement schedule against the trial balance — identifying missing account codes, correcting discrepancies, and ensuring every project's closing balance agrees with the trial balance.
Reflection
Working across multiple audit files this week was challenging but highly rewarding. The property development cost review required a great deal of patience, tracing discrepancies line by line and ensuring every figure reconciled correctly. I learned that audit work demands not just technical knowledge but also a methodical and structured approach to problem-solving. I feel more confident in handling working papers and look forward to taking on greater responsibility in the weeks ahead.
Daily Tasks
  • 20 Apr, Sun
    Performed sales cutoff testing as part of audit fieldwork. Extracted and transferred over 100 invoices covering both periods before and after the financial year end date into a structured Excel working paper.

    Cutoff Testing Procedure:
    Reviewed invoices issued in the period immediately before and after the year end to verify that sales transactions were recorded in the correct accounting period. For each invoice, key details including the invoice date, invoice number, and invoice amount were systematically keyed into the Excel cutoff schedule to facilitate further audit analysis and review.

    Purpose of Work:
    The cutoff schedule serves as audit evidence to confirm that revenue recognised before the year end date relates to goods or services delivered within that period, and that no post-year-end transactions were prematurely recorded. Ensuring the completeness and accuracy of the sales figures in the financial statements.
What I Learned
This task introduced me to one of the most important substantive audit procedures, sales cutoff testing. I learned that cutoff is a key audit assertion, ensuring that revenue is recognised in the correct accounting period in accordance with accounting standards. By working through over 100 invoices spanning both sides of the year end, I developed a clearer understanding of how auditors assess whether sales have been recorded too early or too late. I also improved my speed and accuracy in data entry and Excel organisation under real audit conditions.
Reflection
Working through a large volume of invoices required sustained focus and careful attention to detail. I found the task both repetitive and meaningful, each invoice represents a real transaction that must be verified to ensure the financial statements give a true and fair view. This experience has strengthened my understanding of the relationship between audit evidence and financial reporting accuracy. I look forward to completing the cutoff analysis and seeing how the findings feed into the overall audit conclusion.
