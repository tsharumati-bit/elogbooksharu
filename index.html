  • 02 Apr, Wed

  • 03 Apr, Thu
    Continued audit report casting and conducted market value assessments of properties (Bricks) — assisted in verifying and documenting the market value of property assets as part of the audit evidence.

What I Learned
This week I gained practical exposure to two core audit processes — casting and client review. Casting taught me the importance of numerical accuracy in financial reporting; even a small discrepancy must be identified and resolved before the report is finalised. The market value assessment work introduced me to how auditors verify non-financial assets such as property, and the importance of obtaining reliable valuation evidence.
Reflection
Starting my first week at UHY Penang was both exciting and humbling. The pace of real audit work is faster than I expected — attention to detail is critical at every step. I initially found casting tedious, but I now understand its importance in ensuring the integrity of the final audit report. I look forward to deepening my understanding of audit methodology in the coming weeks.
Daily Tasks
  • 08 Apr, Tue
    Prepared audit working papers — worked on two main files: the General Ledger (GL) extract and the Revenue Lead Schedule.General Ledger (GL):Extracted all transactions under account (Misc Income – Upkeep, Maintenance & Security) from the main ledger and organised them into a dedicated worksheet. Performed a company-level analysis identifying tenants along with their transaction counts, total credits and total debits — flagging naming inconsistencies.Revenue Lead Schedule:Updated the Factory Rental section with current year invoice dates and invoice numbers by cross-referencing GL data for all tenants.
  • 07 Apr – 10 Apr
    Reviewed and updated the Property Development Cost working paper.Missing Account Codes:Identified account codes recorded in the trial balance that were not yet reflected in the working paper and added the missing codes under their respective project groups.Reconciliation & Corrections:Traced each discrepancy to specific account codes and corrected the values to ensure totals reconcile.Movement Schedule Completeness:Verified that all additions, reclassifications, and reversals were properly captured.
What I Learned
This week deepened my understanding of audit working paper preparation across two key areas. From the GL and Revenue Lead Schedule work, I learned how rental income is systematically traced from the general ledger to the lead schedule. The Property Development Cost working paper taught me how auditors reconcile a movement schedule against the trial balance, identifying missing account codes and correcting discrepancies.
Reflection
Working across multiple audit files this week was challenging but highly rewarding. The property development cost review required a great deal of patience tracing discrepancies line by line. I learned that audit work demands not just technical knowledge but also a methodical and structured approach to problem-solving.

What I Learned
This week gave me exposure to two very different but equally important aspects of audit work. The document management tasks taught me that proper organisation of physical evidence is critical to audit efficiency. The sales cutoff testing introduced me to one of the most important substantive audit procedures — the cutoff assertion ensures revenue is recognised in the correct accounting period.
Reflection
This week reminded me that audit work involves both analytical and organisational skills — both are equally important in delivering quality work. The sales cutoff work required sustained focus while handling a large volume of invoices; each invoice represents a real transaction that must be verified to ensure the financial statements give a true and fair view.
Daily Tasks
  • 22 Apr – 24 Apr, Tue–Thu
    Field Work — Juru Volvo Company, Juru

    Attended field work at a Volvo car dealership in Juru as part of the audit engagement. Performed vouching of sales transactions — traced recorded car sales in the accounting system back to source documents including official receipts and sales invoices to verify the completeness, accuracy, and validity of revenue.

    Vouching Procedure — Sales of Vehicles: For each sampled vehicle sale, cross-referenced the sales entry in the ledger against the corresponding official receipt issued to the customer and the sales invoice raised by the dealership. Verified that the sale amount, date, vehicle details, and customer particulars were consistent across all documents. Checked that all receipts and invoices were properly authorised and sequentially numbered with no unexplained gaps. Audit Objective: The vouching exercise provided assurance over the occurrence assertion — confirming that recorded sales transactions actually took place and were supported by valid documentary evidence. It also tested the accuracy assertion by ensuring the amounts recorded agreed to the underlying receipts and invoices.
  • 25 Apr, Fri
    Returned to the UHY office to organise and document the field work findings — compiled the completed vouching schedule, cross-referenced any exceptions noted during field work, and prepared a summary of the results for senior review.
What I Learned
This was my first experience attending actual field work at a client's premises, and it was an eye-opening week. Vouching car sales at Juru Volvo taught me how auditors gather primary evidence directly from source documents — receipts and invoices are the backbone of substantive testing. I learned that the vouching process is not just about matching numbers; it also involves checking for authorisation, completeness of document trails, and consistency of details. Seeing audit work happen at the client site gave me a much more concrete understanding of how audit procedures connect to real business operations.
Reflection
Going out to the field was a highlight of my internship so far. Working at the Volvo dealership in Juru allowed me to experience audit in a completely different environment — outside the office, at the client's premises, going through physical documents in a real business setting. It required a high level of focus and organisation to work through the volume of receipts and invoices efficiently. I felt a great sense of pride and confidence completing my first field work assignment, and it has motivated me to approach the remaining weeks of my internship with even greater dedication and enthusiasm.
