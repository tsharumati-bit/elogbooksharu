What I Learned
This week I gained practical exposure to two core audit processes casting and client review. Casting taught me the importance of numerical accuracy in financial reporting; even a small discrepancy must be identified and resolved before the report is finalised. The market value assessment work introduced me to how auditors verify non-financial assets such as property, and the importance of obtaining reliable valuation evidence.
Reflection
Starting my first week at UHY Penang was both exciting and humbling. The pace of real audit work is faster than I expected attention to detail is critical at every step. I initially found casting tedious, but I now understand its importance in ensuring the integrity of the final audit report. I look forward to deepening my understanding of audit methodology in the coming weeks.
Daily Tasks
  • 08 Apr, Tue
    Prepared audit working papers for Shantawood Sdn Bhd (FYE 31 March 2026) — worked on two main files: the General Ledger (GL) extract and the Revenue Lead Schedule (41533).

    General Ledger (STW GL):
    Extracted all transactions under account 5700/002 (Misc Income – Upkeep, Maintenance & Security) from the main STW ledger and organised them into a dedicated worksheet following the same format as existing GL sub-schedules. Organised the 5700/003 (Factory Rental) worksheet with proper structured formatting including SUM formula totals rows. Performed a company-level analysis of account 5700/003, identifying 16 tenants along with their transaction counts, total credits and total debits — flagging naming inconsistencies for Hong Yun Aluminium across multiple entries.

    Revenue Lead Schedule (41533-2):
    Updated the 5700/003 Factory Rental section with current year (FY2026) invoice dates and invoice numbers by cross-referencing STW GL data for all tenants. Ensured invoice details, monthly rental amounts, duration (months), and total rental calculations were consistent with GL entries. Maintained all existing formulas, formatting, and cross-sheet references throughout both files.
What I Learned
This task gave me hands-on experience in audit working paper preparation — a core skill in external auditing. I learned how General Ledger sub-schedules are structured and how auditors extract and organise transaction data by account code for review. The Revenue Lead Schedule work helped me understand how rental income is traced from the GL to the lead schedule, and the importance of ensuring every figure ties back accurately. Identifying the naming inconsistency for Hong Yun Aluminium also showed me how even small discrepancies in data must be flagged during audit fieldwork.
Reflection
Working on a real client file for the first time was both challenging and rewarding. The level of detail required in audit working papers is much higher than I expected — every formula, format, and figure must be consistent and traceable. I had to be very careful not to break any existing cross-sheet references while making updates. This experience has strengthened my Excel skills and deepened my appreciation for the precision that audit work demands. I look forward to taking on more complex working papers as my confidence grows.
